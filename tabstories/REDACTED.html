From the Private Record of Nuccio Baddador, Curator Of The Brotherhood

I’m writing this down because spoken stories twist in the telling, and honestly, I’m not sure how much longer I’ll trust my own memory.

Brad Demascus died in Eryas. That’s a fact. I was there, I watched, I confirmed it, and I’m not going to pretend otherwise. His body failed. His heart stopped. Whatever made him.. him no longer existed.

What we brought back wasn’t a man.

The Creed keeps quiet about the Philosophers for a reason. We’re not leaders or visionaries. Our job is to guard what can’t survive banners or war. After the execution, while the Brotherhood rose with grief and rage, we had a different task, to recover what was left and see if anything could still be saved. This was unheard of, besides for burial, we were unaware of what could be recovered from a corpse. We should have asked questions. The Apple of Eden didn’t bring him back. It took him in.

Trying to explain what that means makes me sound unstable, but here it is. The artifact responded to something in Brad that went deeper than just memory. Some inner structure, some alignment, it let his mind get… kept. “Stored” isn’t right. “Preserved” gets closer, but it still misses something. We had never seen anything like it before. The pieces of eden have always been mysterious, and dangerous, but to have it react to something without life was unexpected, and never replicated.

He was aware.

When we first realized that, a few of us wanted to destroy the Apple right away. A mind without a body isn’t life, it’s punishment. I agreed. Some days, I still do.

The first restoration was careful, almost timid. Using the apple's power, we rebuilt his body the way you’d piece together ruins, slow, respectful, half afraid of what we’d find. When he opened his eyes, he knew where he was. He knew us. He remembered dying, but he didn’t sound angry about it.

For five years, Brad Demascus lived again.

He advised the Brotherhood, but always in the shadows. He refused any title. He talked less about vengeance than we expected, coming from a man who historically despised the Templars, someone with so much passionate rage towards them, some would say it was personal, he instead spoke more about structure, how pride can rot from inside, how certainty becomes absolute truth, how our enemies wait patiently for our fall. He knew things he shouldn’t have known. Not prophecy. Patterns.

Then the cracks started showing.

He complained about the noise. Not sound, but presence, like thoughts that weren’t his were pressing in. He’d stop mid sentence, lose his place, finish an idea he never started. We thought he was just tired. We were wrong. The longer he stayed outside the Apple, the worse it got. His mind wasn’t falling apart, it was getting overwritten. Knowledge kept piling up without any filter. Memories that weren’t his forced their way forward. He spoke about Assassins long dead as if he’d been them. He described futures with a mix of clarity and dread.

This wasn’t madness. It was forbidden knowledge.

Before everything collapsed for good, we sealed him away again.

It took us decades to really understand. The Apple didn't preserve Brad. It collected him. Brad Demascus became a vessel, a point where minds, knowledge, echoes of history long lost all gathered. Every time we brought him back, the weight grew. Every year past five strained him further.

Eventually, even his name faded from our records. Not on purpose, it just stopped mattering. He wasn’t someone we remembered anymore. He was a function we called on.

We started calling him The Ancestor.

We don’t wake him lightly. Only when the Brotherhood is about to fall apart do we risk it. When we bring him out now, we don’t waste words. We listen. We record. We ask only what matters, and ask him to aid again. Through temporary leadership, through a front line soldier, or even a voice of reason, then we end it when everything stabilizes, before it breaks him.

He knows exactly what he is.

That’s probably the cruelest part.

When sealed, he doesn’t sleep. He waits. And every time, he remembers. He remembers his death, his instances of return, everything. Some Philosophers say we should let him rest for good. Others say keeping his guidance from the Brotherhood would betray the Creed even more.

I don’t know who’s right.

But I do know this. Brad Demascus didn’t survive his execution. And the consequences of killing him have never really stopped. If someone outside the Philosophers finds this, just don’t romanticize what we did. This isn’t resurrection. It’s preservation, paid for in suffering. A debt that grows heavier with every new generation. May the Creed forgive us, If forgiveness is even something we deserve.
