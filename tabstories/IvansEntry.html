Private record of Ivan Wolfe, Captain of Sentinels. Released by Farseer Arch.

With the execution of Commodore Brad, our ranks have grown uneasy in ways I did not anticipate. The fall of a leader of his stature has done more than demoralize us.. It has fractured us. Those who would act with confidence now hesitate. Orders are followed, but they feel as if they lack conviction. Conversation is quiet when officers enter the room. Confidence is now performative.

This doubt is not reserved for the recruits alone. It shows amongst our leadership as well. Hell— even I feel it. I never believed I would write those words.

Brad’s optimism is what first made me care for Sentinels beyond just my assigned tasks. He had a way of speaking about our future as if it was already set in stone; as if success was already in our hands.

Now he is dead.

Optimism did not save him.

I see the change in how our officers act. Some have begun to embrace his ideals as if repeating them will resurrect him. Others grow restless, already plotting to use this for their own gains. Both paths are a danger. One refuses to see reality while the other seeks opportunity in chaos.

I cannot allow either to define us.

If doubt continues to spread we will not have to worry about the threats against our Creed. We will fracture from within. Brad led with hope, I respected him for it. But hope, I am beginning to understand, is a fragile foundation for command.

Someone must replace it with someone stronger.

Certainty.

Even if that certainty must be forced.

I do not enjoy the thought. But leadership is not about comfort.. It’s about survival. And if survival demands that I step up then so be it.

Brad inspired loyalty.

I will demand it.
