Origins and Philosophy

The Assassins Brotherhood traces its origins back to the dawn of civilization, emerging from the shadows as a clandestine group dedicated to safeguarding the freedoms and rights of individuals. The roots of the Brotherhood lie in the ancient civilizations of Mesopotamia, Egypt, and Persia, where the first Assassins operated in secret, protecting the vulnerable and challenging oppressive rulers.

Founding Principles The Assassins were founded on the principles of freedom and the protection of the innocent. They believed that every individual has the right to make their own choices and live without the constraints imposed by tyrannical authorities. This belief set them apart from the Templars, who sought to impose their vision of order and control upon the world. The Assassins saw themselves as the defenders of free will, ensuring that humanity could shape its own destiny.

The Creed The Brotherhood's creed, "Nothing is true, everything is permitted," is a philosophical statement that encapsulates their worldview. This creed reflects their understanding that reality is shaped by perception and that societal norms and rules are often constructs designed to control the masses. By acknowledging this, the Assassins embrace a form of enlightened skepticism, freeing themselves from the constraints of dogma and allowing them to think and act independently.

  1. "Nothing is true" – This part of the creed emphasizes the importance of questioning everything. The Assassins believe that to achieve true understanding and enlightenment, one must be willing to challenge accepted truths and look beyond appearances. This skepticism allows them to see through the deceptions and manipulations of those in power.
  2. "Everything is permitted" – This principle underscores the idea that with understanding comes the responsibility of action. The Assassins believe that individuals must be free to act according to their own conscience and judgment, unrestrained by arbitrary laws and moral codes. However, this freedom is tempered by a sense of duty to protect the innocent and uphold justice.

Early Days and Key Figures In the early days, the Brotherhood was led by visionaries who laid the foundation for their enduring legacy. Among them was Darius, a Persian Assassin who was instrumental in the formation of the Brotherhood. He was known for his formidable skills and unwavering commitment to the cause of freedom. Darius is credited with the creation of the hidden blade, a signature weapon that allowed Assassins to strike swiftly and silently.

Another key figure was Amunet, an Egyptian Assassin who played a pivotal role in the Brotherhood's efforts to resist the rule of oppressive pharaohs. Her leadership and cunning strategies helped to liberate many from the chains of servitude, solidifying the Brotherhood's reputation as protectors of the downtrodden.

Spreading Influence As the Brotherhood grew, it spread its influence across the ancient world, establishing cells in major cities and regions. They operated in the shadows, gathering intelligence, thwarting plots of tyrants, and eliminating those who posed a threat to freedom. The Assassins relied on a network of informants, spies, and allies to aid their cause, creating an intricate web of connections that allowed them to act swiftly and effectively.

Conflict with the Templars The Assassins' philosophy inevitably brought them into conflict with the Templars, a group equally ancient but diametrically opposed in their goals. The Templars believed in imposing order through control and sought to create a world where peace was achieved by eliminating chaos and dissent. This fundamental difference in ideology set the stage for centuries of conflict between the two factions.

The Assassins and Templars clashed across the ages, from the Crusades in the Holy Land to the Renaissance in Europe. Each side vied for control of powerful artifacts known as Pieces of Eden, which were said to possess the ability to shape human destiny. These artifacts became focal points of their struggle, with the Assassins seeking to keep them out of the hands of those who would use them to subjugate humanity.
