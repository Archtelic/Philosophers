The Brotherhood has always stood in opposition to the Templars. Their first major confrontation occurred during the Crusades, where the Assassins fought to protect the Holy Land from falling into the hands of those who sought to control it for their own ends. Under the leadership of Al Mualim, the Brotherhood grew in strength and numbers, perfecting the art of stealth, deception, and strategic assassination.

The roots of the conflict between the Assassins and the Templars can be traced back to the very foundations of both orders. The Templars, believing in imposing order through control and absolute power, aimed to shape the world according to their vision. In stark contrast, the Assassins cherished individual freedom and sought to protect humanity from the shackles of tyranny. These opposing ideologies inevitably led to clashes as both factions sought to assert their influence over the rapidly changing world.

The Crusades marked the first major confrontation between the Assassins and the Templars. The Holy Land, a region of immense religious and strategic significance, became the epicenter of their conflict. The Templars, seeking to establish dominion over the region, clashed with the Assassins, who were determined to protect it from falling under oppressive control.

Under the leadership of Al Mualim, the Assassins orchestrated a series of covert operations to undermine the Templar forces. Al Mualim, a master strategist and charismatic leader, recognized the importance of intelligence and subterfuge in their struggle. He emphasized the need for his followers to blend into the shadows, gather information, and strike only when the moment was right.

One of the most significant events during the Crusades was the Siege of Morsera. The Templars, led by their Grand Master, launched a formidable assault on the Assassins' mountain fortress. Morsera, strategically located and well-fortified, became a symbol of resistance. The Assassins, using their intimate knowledge of the terrain and guerrilla tactics, managed to repel the Templars. Though the battle resulted in the absolute destruction of Morsera. The siege demonstrated the Brotherhood's resilience and their ability to defend their stronghold against overwhelming odds.

The conflict between the Assassins and the Templars during the Crusades was characterized by a hidden war fought in the shadows. While large-scale battles raged across the Holy Land, the Assassins engaged in a series of covert operations. They targeted key Templar figures, sabotaged supply lines, and gathered intelligence on their enemies' plans. This hidden war required exceptional skill, patience, and an unwavering commitment to the Brotherhood's cause.

Al Mualim's leadership was instrumental in shaping the Brotherhood's tactics and philosophy during the Crusades. He instilled a sense of purpose and unity among the Assassins, emphasizing the importance of their creed. Al Mualim believed that their fight was not just against the Templars but against the very idea of control and oppression. His teachings and strategies laid the foundation for the Brotherhood's enduring legacy.
